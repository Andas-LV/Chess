World Cup

The tournament is open to players of all nationalities and features a knockout format. The first Chess World Cup was held in 1988, and since then it has become a regular fixture in the international chess calendar. The Chess World Cup is usually held every two years, with the winner of the tournament earning a spot in the Candidates Tournament, which is the final stage of the World Chess Championship cycle. The winner of the Candidates Tournament then goes on to challenge the reigning World Chess Champion for the title. The Chess World Cup is known for attracting many of the world's top chess players, including current World Chess Champion Magnus Carlsen. Grand Prix

The Chess Grand Prix is a series of chess tournaments organized by the World Chess Federation (FIDE) as part of its efforts to determine the challenger for the World Chess Championship. The series consists of several tournaments held over a two-year period, with players earning points based on their performance in each tournament. The top players in the Grand Prix series qualify for the Candidates Tournament, which determines the challenger for the World Chess Championship. The Grand Prix series is one of several paths for players to qualify for the Candidates Tournament, with the others including the World Cup and the Ratings Grand Prix.

The most recent edition of the Chess Grand Prix was held from 2019 to 2020, with tournaments taking place in Moscow, Riga, Hamburg, Jerusalem, and Palma de Mallorca.

Championship

The Chess Championship is the highest level of competition in the game of chess, where the world's best players compete for the title of World Chess Champion. The championship is organized by the World Chess Federation (FIDE) and is held every two years. The winner of the championship is considered the strongest chess player in the world. The championship is typically a match between the reigning champion and a challenger who qualifies through various events such as the Candidates Tournament, the World Chess Grand Prix, or the World Cup. The format of the championship varies, but it usually involves a series of games played over several weeks, with the first player to reach a certain number of wins being declared the champion.
